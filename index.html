How I approach quality QA strategy

Quality as a shared responsibility: from acceptance criteria and exploratory testing to automation, CI/CD and continuous feedback loops.

QA approach in projects

  • Test strategy definition based on risk, impact and business criticality.
  • Test design with clear, traceable, and reusable test cases (TestRail / Zephyr).
  • Pragmatic automation: focus on critical regression and core business flows.
  • Exploratory testing to uncover edge cases, UX gaps and integration issues.
  • CI/CD integration with automated suites and quality gates for early feedback.
  • Defect management prioritised by real user impact and business risk.

Types of tests I typically combine

  • Functional and regression testing on core business flows.
  • Automated smoke tests to quickly validate new builds.
  • Basic performance checks (Jmeter / LoadRunner) on key endpoints or flows.
  • Accessibility checks on UI components and content.
  • Integration validation (e.g. Salesforce and external services).
  • Log and metrics analysis as part of defect investigation.

Key aspects checklist for a QA engineer

  • Clear requirements: Acceptance criteria understood and agreed across QA and Dev.
  • Regression coverage: Core business flows covered, ideally automated.
  • Exploratory testing: Scheduled sessions to discover edge cases and UX issues.
  • Quality in CI/CD: Automated suites integrated into pipelines with explicit quality gates.
  • Test data: Strategy for safe, repeatable test data creation and cleanup.
  • Performance basics: Validate response times and stability for representative scenarios.
  • Accessibility: Check contrast, keyboard navigation and alternative texts.
  • Defect management: Well‑documented bugs, prioritised by impact and actively followed up.
  • Communication: Clear, actionable quality status shared with stakeholders.
  • Continuous learning: Post‑release feedback loop to adjust strategy and prevent regressions.
